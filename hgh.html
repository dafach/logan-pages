1-2 IU/day — Replacement / Longevity

Low▶

Timeline

  • Week 1-2: Better sleep quality (often first thing noticed)
  • Week 4-6: More energy, faster workout recovery
  • Month 2-3: Skin elasticity improves, nails/hair quality up
  • Month 3-6: Subtle body composition shift, reduced joint stiffness

Benefits

  • Deeper slow-wave sleep
  • Better skin thickness, elasticity, hydration
  • Faster recovery from training and minor injuries
  • Gradual visceral fat reduction
  • Improved mood and cognitive sharpness

Side Effects

  • Minimal. Occasional mild water retention first 2-4 weeks
  • Fasting glucose: may bump 2-5 mg/dL initially, often normalizes
  • Long-term low-dose GH can actually improve insulin sensitivity through visceral fat reduction

Community

"The sweet spot for anti-aging if you're 40+"
"Changes are subtle week to week but obvious over months"
"A dose many people run long-term"

Protocol

  • Single injection — evening or morning fasted
  • 5 on/2 off or daily — both work at this dose
  • Can run indefinitely with monitoring
  • Monitor IGF-1 and fasting glucose every 6 months

2-4 IU/day — The Sweet Spot

Moderate▶

Timeline

  • Week 2-4: Water retention peaks then subsides. Energy boost.
  • Month 1-2: Fat loss becomes noticeable, especially midsection
  • Month 2-4: Significant recomp. Visibly "cleaner" — more defined, not necessarily bigger.
  • Month 4-6: Full fat loss and body comp effects realized

Benefits

  • Significant fat loss, particularly visceral/abdominal
  • Lean mass retention during caloric deficit
  • Accelerated wound healing and injury recovery
  • Enhanced collagen synthesis (joints, tendons)
  • Improved lipid profiles (decreased LDL, total cholesterol)
  • Fuller, denser muscle appearance

Side Effects

  • Water retention: moderate first 3-6 weeks (hands, face). Subsides.
  • Carpal tunnel: 5-10% of users. Numbness/tingling, worse at night.
  • Joint stiffness: 10-15% incidence
  • Fasting glucose: +5-15 mg/dL. HbA1c +0.1-0.2%.
  • All reversible with dose reduction

Community

"4 IU is the go-to bodybuilding dose"
"HGH allows you to GROW LEAN — less time dieting, more time building"
"Keeps me stupid lean no matter what I eat"
"3 IU on cruise, 4 IU on blast — great results"

Protocol

  • At 2-3 IU: single injection (AM fasted or PM pre-bed)
  • At 4 IU: split — 2 IU AM fasted + 2 IU PM pre-bed
  • Always inject in low-insulin state (2+ hrs after eating)
  • 5 on/2 off or daily
  • Jay Campbell: max 6 months, then 2-3 month break
  • Monitor fasting glucose, HbA1c, IGF-1 every 3 months

4-6 IU/day — Aggressive Recomp

High▶

Timeline

  • Week 1-4: Water retention, possible carpal tunnel, energy shifts
  • Month 1-2: Strength increases noticeable
  • Month 2-4: Visible muscle gains and fat loss, enhanced vascularity
  • Month 4-6: Peak recomp. Dramatic fullness and muscle density.

Benefits

  • Lean muscle gains: 1-3 kg over 16 weeks with TRT (clinical data)
  • Strength: 14-35% improvement (clinical trials)
  • Major fat loss (1-2+ kg fat mass reduction)
  • Improved tendon/ligament integrity
  • Dramatic fullness and vascularity

Side Effects — Escalate Here

  • Water retention: significant. Puffy face, swollen extremities.
  • Carpal tunnel: 15-25%. Can persist 2-3 months after dropping dose.
  • Insulin resistance: clinically significant. FBG +10-20 mg/dL. HbA1c +0.2-0.3%.
  • Blood pressure: ~12/8 mmHg increase
  • Thyroid: GH increases T4→T3 conversion. Monitor.
  • Possible gynecomastia, digestive sluggishness

Community

"Reserved for experienced users"
"Risk/benefit tips past the sweet spot for non-competitive users"

Protocol

  • Split dosing mandatory: 2-3 IU AM + 2-3 IU PM
  • 3-4 months on, 1-2 months off
  • 5 on/2 off during active cycles
  • Monitor every 3 months minimum
  • Diet quality, sleep, carb timing all become critical

6-10+ IU/day — Pro Bodybuilding

Extreme▶

Not a longevity dose. Not a lifestyle dose. Most elite users cycle in and out of this range. They do not live here.

Side Effects — Serious

  • Insulin resistance / T2 diabetes: major risk. Many run exogenous insulin.
  • Carpal tunnel: 30%+ incidence
  • Acromegaly features: jaw, hands, feet, brow ridge (chronic use)
  • "GH gut": organ/intestinal hypertrophy
  • Cardiovascular: hypertension, cardiac hypertrophy
  • Cancer risk: chronically elevated IGF-1 linked to increased mortality

Protocol

  • Split into 2-3 injections, all fasted
  • Max 3-4 months on, min 2-3 months off
  • Requires concurrent metabolic management

Blood Sugar — The Real Gatekeeper

Critical▶
  • "HGH causes diabetes" is overstated — it temporarily reduces insulin sensitivity. In healthy people, adaptive and reversible. In unhealthy people, exposes existing dysfunction.
  • Insulin sensitivity determines your GH experience. Leaner = better. Sleep deprivation worsens it. Diet quality matters more than dose.
  • Onset isn't always gradual. One user: "You're humming along fine, then bang, literally overnight." Another: 85→115 mg/dL over 9 months on just 2 IU.
  • GLP-1 agonists are the game-changer. Tirzepatide directly counteracts GH-induced insulin resistance.
  • Mitigation stack: Berberine, metformin, ALA, cardio, staying lean, clean diet.
  • You don't need insulin at low-moderate doses. That's a bodybuilding myth. Insulin misuse introduces far more risk.

Injection Timing

Guide▶

Morning Fasted — Best for Fat Loss

  • Enhanced lipolysis, especially with fasted cardio 15-30 min later
  • Jay Campbell: no food 30 min before, 1.5 hrs after
  • Downside: many report afternoon sleepiness/crash

Bedtime — Best for Recovery / Anti-Aging

  • Mimics natural nocturnal GH spike
  • Better sleep quality reported by most
  • SubQ peaks ~4 hrs post-injection — still provides next-day fat loss benefits
  • Counterargument: may interfere with natural GH release during sleep

Split Dose — Best for 4+ IU

  • Half AM fasted + half PM pre-bed
  • Reduces side effects vs single large dose
  • More stable IGF-1 levels
"You'll get benefits pinning regardless of time of day." Consistency > timing.

Stacking Interactions

Synergy▶

GH + TRT — Strong Synergy

  • Near-unanimous: GH works dramatically better with a testosterone base
  • Testosterone enhances GH→IGF-1 conversion — may need less GH on TRT
  • 2-4 IU GH + TRT is the standard stack
"HGH was the key to unlocking my TRT"
"To use GH alone without an anabolic base would be a mistake"

GH + Tirzepatide/GLP-1 — "The Holy Trinity" (with TRT)

  • GH increases insulin resistance → tirzepatide counteracts it. Key synergy.
  • GH can restore some appetite on GLP-1s (feature, not bug)
  • TRT + HGH + GLP-1 is being called "the holy trinity"
"I seem to be a metabolic powerhouse on this stack" — test 250 + GH 6 IU + reta 10mg
Test 120 + reta 8mg + GH 2→4 IU: 255 to 200 lbs over 8 months, strength still progressing

GH + Peptide Stacking

  • Low-dose GH (1-2 IU) + secretagogues for combined amplification
  • Peptides during GH off-cycles to maintain some elevation
  • CJC-1295 (no DAC) + Ipamorelin = "gold standard" combo (2-5x GH release)

Where Sources Agree vs Disagree

Analysis▶

Agreement

  • Sleep improvement is earliest and most consistent benefit
  • 2-4 IU is the sweet spot for most non-competitive users
  • Blood sugar is the primary risk to manage
  • GH works dramatically better with a testosterone base
  • Split dosing is better at 4+ IU
  • Side effects are dose-dependent and mostly reversible
  • GLP-1 agonists are the ideal co-therapy for metabolic protection

Disagreement

  • Timing: AM vs PM genuinely debated with good arguments both ways
  • Cycling at low doses: Some say indefinite is fine; others insist on breaks
  • Secretagogues vs real GH: AntiDoc says C-tier (pointless). Elevate sees them as complementary. Jay Campbell includes both.
  • IGF-1 targets: Wide range of opinions on optimal levels
  • Cancer risk: Attia emphasizes IGF-1/cancer link; bodybuilding community largely dismisses it
